About Us

This society aims to encourage discussion of the latest developments in the cognitive science of culture, to provide a platform for exchanging ideas, and a network to foster collaborations among interested researchers. We want to understand the human mind and how cultural objects (e.g., literacy; religion), including those within culture at macro-level, can shape the human brain and mind.

Geetha Bakilapadavu

BITS Pilani K.K. Birla Goa Campus, India

Geetha Bakilapadavu is an Associate Professor at BITS Pilani, K. K. Birla Goa Campus. She has been working in interdisciplinary areas of research and is adept at finding ways to make interdisciplinary connections. Her Doctoral research was in the area of interphase between Science Fiction and Philosophy. She is a recipient of Science Fiction Foundation, Liverpool Bursary Award. She was a visiting scholar at Georgia Tech University, Atlanta in the year 2015. Her current research interests are Film and Cognition, Digital Humanities, and Creative Pedagogies. She has been offering courses in the areas of Film Studies, Literature, Film Making, and Humanities & Design. Apart from journal publications in the area of education, literature and film studies, she has edited an anthology of essays titled Exploring Science Fiction: Text and Pedagogy. She nurtures creative interests, has published poems and has been working towards a musical documentary film.

Jon Andoni Duñabeitia

Nebrija University, Spain

Jon Andoni Duñabeitia Ph.D. is a researcher in Cognitive Science of Language who currently works as a Full Professor at the Facultad de Lenguas y Educación of the Universidad Nebrija. Prof. Duñabeitia is the Director of the Centro de Ciencia Cognitiva (C3) and he is the Principal Investigator of the research group CEDI (Cognición, Educación y Diferencias Individuales). The main research interests of Jon Andoni Duñabeitia focus on the study of the neurobiological and cognitive substrates of multilingualism and reading throughout the life cycle. He aims to understand the cognitive processes associated with the acquisition of oral and written languages in monolingual and multilingual populations of different ages.

Tania Fernandes

University of Lisbon, Portugal

Tani Fernandes is an Assistant Professor and Principal Investigator of the Research Group Cognition in Context (CO2, Center for Research in Psychological Sciences). My major scientific interest is to understand how cultural experiences can modulate the cognitive system. My scientific work has been conducted in the field of Cognitive Psychology, taking into account cognitive neuropsychological and neuroscience perspectives, on Speech Perception, and on the impact of learning to read in evolutionary older cognitive systems, that is, in visual object recognition and in oral language.

Alexis Hervais-Adelman

University of Zurich, Switzerland

Alexis Hervais-Adelman is head of the Neurolinguistics Division at the Psychology Institute of the University of Zurich. His research uses several approaches to elucidate the brain networks of language, and interventions that might serve to train and improve them. His team deploys multiple neuroimaging and behavioural methods methods to investigate the brain networks of extreme language - be it the perception of acoustically challenging speech, or the challenges posed by multilingualism.

Regine Kolinsky

Université Libre de Bruxelles, Belgium

She has been an invited Professor in Brazilian and Japanese universities (Human Brain Research Center, Kyoto University). Prof. Jubin Abutalebi

University San Raffaele, Milan, Italy

Prof. Jubin Abutalebi, MD (Italy), PhD (Hong Kong) born in Vienna (Austria) is a cognitive neurologist and professor of neuropsychology at the University San Raffaele in Milan, Italy. He is worldwide known for his studies addressing the cognitive neuroscience of bilingualism, neurolinguistics and neuroimaging. Jubin Abutalebi is the editor-in-chief of the prestigious international journal “Bilingualism: Language and Cognition” (Cambridge University Press). He is also honorary professor at the University of Hong Kong, and affiliated stuff member at the Higher School of Economics in Moscow, Russia.

Suvarna Alladi

National Institute of Mental Health and Neuro Sciences, Bengaluru, India

Dr Suvarna is working as a Professor for Neurology, India at the National Institute of Mental Health and Neuro Sciences. She has a wide range of experience. She is also associated with the Alzheimer's and Related Disorders Society of India. She has completed her Postgraduate Certification in Cognitive Ageing Research Methods for Medical Scientists, University of Edinburgh (2012) and Commonwealth Academic Staff Fellowship in Cognitive Neurology at the University of Cambridge (2006). Her international experience includes various programs, contributions and participation in different countries for diverse fields of study. Her research interests reflect in his wide range of publications in various national and international journals. She has a wide range of interest in the fields of Cognitive Neurology, Alzheimer's disease, Frontotemporal dementia syndromes, Vascular Cognitive Impairment, Bilingualism and Cognition, Risk and protective factors for dementia, the Vision-action-language interface in neurology.
